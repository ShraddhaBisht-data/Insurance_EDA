# Insurance_EDA
# 🏥 Medical Insurance Cost Analysis

##  Overview

This project analyzes medical insurance costs to understand which factors increase healthcare expenses and how insurance companies can use data for better decision-making.

---

#  Objectives

* Analyze factors affecting insurance charges
* Identify high-risk customers
* Understand customer behavior patterns
* Generate business insights using data analysis

---

#  Initial Hypotheses

1. Smokers have higher insurance charges
2. Higher BMI increases medical costs
3. Older people pay more insurance charges
4. Customers with children may have higher expenses
5. Insurance charges vary by region

---

#  Data Cleaning

✔ Checked missing values
✔ Removed duplicates
✔ Fixed inconsistencies
✔ Verified data types

---

#  Analysis Performed

## 🔹 Univariate Analysis

* Histograms
* Countplots
* Distribution analysis

### Insights

* Most customers are young adults
* Smokers are fewer but pay much higher charges
* Insurance charges are highly uneven

---

##  Bivariate Analysis

* Scatterplots
* Boxplots
* Barplots

### Findings

✔ Smoking strongly increases charges
✔ Age and BMI positively affect insurance cost
✔ High BMI smokers generate maximum expenses

---

##  Multivariate Analysis

* Heatmaps
* GroupBy analysis
* Combined feature analysis

### Deep Insights

* Older smokers are highest-risk customers
* Smoking + obesity creates very high medical costs
* Non-smokers show stable insurance charges

---

#  Business Insights

* Insurance companies can use risk-based pricing
* Wellness programs can reduce future claims
* High-risk customers should be segmented separately
* Preventive healthcare can lower insurance expenses

---

#  Key Conclusions

✔ Smoking is the biggest cost-driving factor
✔ BMI and age significantly impact charges
✔ Data analysis helps improve pricing strategies
✔ Customer segmentation improves business decisions
